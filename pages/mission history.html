Mission History

Since the dawn of human curiosity, the stars have been our ultimate destination. From the first telescope to interstellar exploration, our missions have expanded our understanding of the universe.

Our first manned mission in the early 21st century marked a revolutionary step in space exploration. With advanced technologies and international collaboration, we launched multiple deep-space probes, lunar stations, and Mars habitats.

These milestones were more than just technological achievements; they represented humanity's boldest dreams taking shape across the cosmos.

Detailed Mission Timeline

Here's a closer look at the most notable missions:

  • 2028: Permanent lunar base established.
  • 2032: First human presence on Mars.
  • 2035: Deep Space Gateway construction.
  • 2040: AI-driven probes beyond asteroid belt.
  • 2045: Mars self-sustaining colonies.
